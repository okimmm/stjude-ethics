FIlipino Culture:

Culture

TRADITIONAL VALUES
- Paggalang (Respect): Deep regard for others, especially elders, demonstrated through polite language and gestures. - Pakikisama (Helping Others): The importance of harmony and cooperation, often involving collective efforts to support one another. - Utang na Loob (Debt of Gratitude): Acknowledgment of favors or help received, leading to a sense of obligation to repay kindness. But this trait have bad effects on some situations because people use this as their advantage to justify a person. - Pagpapahalaga sa Pamilya (Prioritizing the Family): Strong focus on family loyalty and support, often placing family needs above individual desires. Especially when Filos have financial problems they tend to help their own family to sustain on daily lives. - Hiya (Shame): A social value that emphasizes self-awareness and the importance of maintaining a good reputation in the eyes of others. Some filos have this trait because they don't want to be judge by others and lead to decreased their self esteem. - Damayan System: A communal support system where members of the community help each other during times of need, such as during illnesses or hardships. Especially on the Calamity Family stick and help together to find solution to survive. - Compassion: A deep sense of empathy and care for others, often leading to acts of kindness and support in times of trouble. Even it is not enough for Filos just to help the needs, and they are happy about it. - Fun Loving Trait: A joyful approach to life, characterized by a love for celebrations, gatherings, and finding happiness even in difficult situations.

Key Points:

  • Smooth Interpersonal Relationships: Respectful and harmonious relationships are a big part of Filipino culture.
  • Amor Propio (Self-esteem): Having a good sense of self-worth is important for keeping social peace, but too much of it can lead to controlling behavior.
